Title: Academic Portal

Date Created: March 2017

Description: I created this application as part of my IBDP Internal Assessment for Computer Science. Back in my school in India, we didn’t have a modern grading system like Canvas—everything was recorded in physical notebooks that professors carried everywhere. To address this, I designed and developed a digital grading portal. The application’s interface was built using JavaScript, and it utilizes HSQLDB as its database. In this portal: Teachers can enter and edit grades, which are securely stored in the database. Students can log in to view their grades but have read-only access. This system was designed to streamline the grading process and make it more efficient for both teachers and students. Check out the demo on the right to see it in action!




Subject Area: Mathematics

Title: Orbit of The Moon

Date Created: May 2017

Description:This project is one of my all-time favorites. As part of my Math HL Internal Assessment in the IBDP, we had to either research a concept or create something new. Being a huge space enthusiast, I chose to research the orbit of the moon. As a child, I was obsessed with the idea of becoming an astronaut, and this project allowed me to explore that passion. I focused on the apogee and perigee of the moon, which, in simple terms, can be thought of as the radius of a circle. Using these concepts, I aimed to derive a formula to calculate the circumference of the moon's orbit. However, there’s a twist—the moon’s orbit doesn’t remain stationary. The universe is constantly in motion (as suggested by the Big Bang Theory), and the moon’s orbit shifts over time due to a phenomenon called apsidal precession—the gradual rotation of the orbit itself. My formula accounts for this dynamic movement, making it a unique approach to understanding the moon’s path through space. You can find a detailed explanation of this concept in the project.

Subject Area: Analytics

Title: Netflix Wrapped

Date Created: April 2024

Description:This is a fun project I worked on after having an idea. I’m a huge fan of cinema—if I could get lost in the world of movies, I absolutely would! Sometimes, I struggle to pick a new show or movie to watch. I realized that understanding my viewing habits could make it much easier to choose. To explore this, I downloaded all my Netflix data, cleaned and organized it by variables like date, genre, and more. Then, I loaded the data into Power BI and created my very own dashboard, which I’ve named Netflix Wrapped (inspired by Spotify Wrapped!). A few fun insights: My most-watched genre is Comedy, and my most-viewed show is The Office. This discovery has made me more confident about choosing comedies because I enjoy lighthearted content and a good laugh. There’s so much more I can do with this dashboard, and it has sparked my desire to delve deeper into Data Science to uncover even more insights in the future.

It was born during a tough period in my life when the future felt uncertain. Being a problem solver, I wanted to create something that could help me understand and navigate my emotions better. I started by tracking my mood daily on a scale from -5 (worst) to 5 (best). I also considered various metrics that could influence mood swings. Each day, I logged my mood into an Excel sheet, creating a cumulative score over time. I then connected this data to Tableau, where I built a visualization to track mood trends. The chart showed how my mood fluctuated and often correlated with events in my life. While this is still in the testing phase and needs extensive research, I’ve collaborated with Psychology students from Columbia University to validate my approach. I believe this tool has immense potential in therapy—therapists could ask patients to log their moods daily, identify patterns, and even use analytics to detect signs of depression or other mental health challenges. For me, personally, it was incredibly rewarding to see my chart improve after a period of lows. It provided a sense of progress when progress wasn’t always easy to feel. Ultimately, my goal is to refine this idea further and help as many people as possible. Whatever I create, I want to ensure it brings joy and positivity to people’s lives.
